DFLpred

A high-throughput predictor of disordered flexible linker (DFL) regions in proteins. It outputs propensity score to form DFL for each residue in the input sequence. This method is characterized by the area under receiver operating characteristic curve (AUC) of 0.715 on independent test set. It needs less than one hour to predict an entire proteome.

  • Webserver: http://biomine-ws.ece.ualberta.ca/DFLpred
  • Standalone package (java): https://fanchi.github.io/DFLpred/
  • Paper: Bioinformatics

    • Analysis of autophagy-related disordered proteins in intra-nuclear compartments

    We collected autophagy-related proteins from the Autophagy Database, and nuclear proteins and their annotations of the intra-nuclear compartments from the Nsort/DB database. By using a majority-vote based prediction of disorder, we analyzed similarities and differences in the intrinsic disorder distribution of nuclear and non-nuclear proteins related to autophagy.

  • Paper: Molecular BioSystems

    • Analysis of intrinsic disorder and protein-protein interactions in Intra-Nuclear Compartments

    We analyzed the profile of intrinsic disorder for 3005 mouse proteins localized in specific intra-nuclear organelles and compared them with 29,863 non-nuclear proteins. Nuclear proteins are collected from the Nsort/DB database, and are mapped into the complete mouse proteome (collected from Uniprot) to create the set of non-nuclear proteins. We also analysed the differences in PPIs (protein-protein interactions) between nuclear proteins (including subsets of nuclear proteins localized in specific intra-nuclear compartments) and non-nuclear proteins by using PPI network collected from the Mentha database.

  • Paper: International Journal of Molecular Sciences

    • Analysis of intrinsic disorder of the dengue virus

    We evaluate the extent of intrinsic disorder in the complete proteomes of genotypes of four human dengue virus, to analyze the peculiarities of disorder distribution within individual DENV proteins, and to establish potential roles for the structural disorder with respect to their functions. The dengue virus proteins are collected from Uniprot, and we use a wide spectrum of bioinformatics techniques to generate 20 types of structural and functional annotations for these proteins.

  • Paper: FEBS Journal

    • bi-BPCA

    A method to impute missing values for microarray data. We find the most correlated genes (rows) and experimental conditions (columns) with the missing entry by biclustering, and impute these biclusters by an existing method, the BPCA (Bayesian Principal Component Analysis) method. An automatic parameter learning scheme is also developed to obtain optimal parameters for this method.

  • Standalone package (Matlab): https://fanchi.github.io/bi-BPCA/
  • Paper: IEEE Journal of Biomedical and Health Informatics
